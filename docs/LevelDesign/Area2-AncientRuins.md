## Area Breakdown

### 1. Ruins Entrance
**Purpose**: Transition from nature to architecture

**Description**:  
Massive stone doorway carved with Guardian symbols. Inside, nature has begun reclaiming the structureâ€”vines, roots, collapsed sections. The Central Spire is visible through gaps in the ceiling.

**Key Elements**:
- Clear visual shift from Grove
- First Ghost Echo (Guardians debating)
- Auto-save checkpoint
- Collapsed floor hint (leads to secret area)
- Path splits: main hall vs. collapsed section

**Environmental Storytelling**:
- Hasty departure evidence (dropped items)
- Defensive positions (were they fighting something?)
- Time erosion (centuries of abandonment)

---

### 2. Entrance Hall - Combat Encounter 1
**Purpose**: First "real" combat test

**Description**:  
A grand entrance hall, now crumbling. Columns provide cover. Blighted wolves patrol the space.

**Combat Encounter**:
- 4 Blighted Wolves
- Patrol patterns (can be observed before engaging)
- Stealth approach possible (thin numbers first)
- Column cover for tactical retreats
- Environmental hazard: unstable column (can be collapsed on enemies)

**Post-Combat**:
- Health drop
- Spirit Essence (2)
- Path forward clears

**Player Learns**:
- Combat is more dangerous now
- Environmental advantages exist
- Observation before engagement helps

---

### 3. Inner Courtyard (Hub)
**Purpose**: Central navigation point, choice in exploration order

**Description**:  
An open courtyard under the sky. The Central Spire rises from its center but is currently inaccessible (blocked by magical barrier). Three paths branch offâ€”all must be completed to lower the barrier.

**Key Elements**:
- Central Spire (goal visible but blocked)
- Three paths: Water Puzzle, Symbol Puzzle, Guardian Homes
- Progress indicator (three lights activate as paths complete)
- Checkpoint
- Lore Stone #5 at spire base

**Player Choice**:
- Can complete paths in any order
- Completing each path activates one of three locks
- All three required to access spire

---

### 6. Guardian Homes (Path C)
**Purpose**: Environmental storytelling, emotional weight

**Description**:  
Residential quarters of the Guardians. Intimate spaces showing how they lived. Combat encounter in the garden outside.

**Environmental Details**:
- Children's rooms (toys, small beds)
- Kitchen with preserved meal setup
- Personal shrines with individual symbols
- Ghost Echoes of daily life

**Combat Encounter 2**:
- Located in garden area
- 3 Blighted Wolves + 1 larger "alpha" wolf
- Alpha has more health, teaches heavy attack importance
- Garden provides limited cover

**Key Discovery**:
- One home has Kira's grandmother's symbol
- Personal artifacts match what Kira carries
- Major story revelation moment

**Rewards**:
- Lore Stone #7 in main home
- Spirit Essence (3) throughout homes
- Third lock mechanism in garden (post-combat)

---

### 7. Council Chamber (After Hub Opens)
**Purpose**: Major lore dump, story progression

**Description**:  
The central governing chamber of the Guardians. Murals cover every wall. A large Lore Stone sits at the center.

**Content**:
- Murals depicting Guardian history
- Decision to allow departures
- The spreading corruption
- The last council meeting

**Key Elements**:
- Lore Stone #8 (Guardian perspective on decline)
- Ghost Echo of final council meeting
- Visual timeline on walls
- Connection to grandmother made explicit

**No Combat**: This is a reflective space.

---

### 8. Archives (Side Path)
**Purpose**: Optional deeper lore, grandmother connection

**Description**:  
Library/archive space. Scrolls, books, preserved knowledge. This is where Elena (grandmother) studied before leaving.

**Key Elements**:
- Ghost Echo of young Elena studying
- Her personal notes (player can examine)
- Lore Stone #9 (Elena's recording)
- Guardian Relic location (puzzle required)

**Optional Puzzle**:
- Bookshelf arrangement puzzle
- Solution found in Elena's notes
- Reveals hidden room with Guardian Relic

**Rewards**:
- Guardian Relic #2
- Spirit Essence (2)
- Deep lore for invested players

## Developer Notes

1. **Hub design is key**: Players should always know where they are relative to the spire
2. **Path order doesn't matter**: All three paths should feel complete on their own
3. **Treant is the test**: This fight should feel earned and challenging
4. **Story revelations matter**: The grandmother connection should land emotionally
5. **Don't rush the homes**: Let players absorb the environmental storytelling
